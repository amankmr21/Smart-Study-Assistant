Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert light energy into chemical energy.
It mainly happens in chloroplasts, where chlorophyll absorbs sunlight.
Plants take in carbon dioxide from the air and water from the soil.
Using sunlight, they produce glucose (a sugar) and release oxygen as a byproduct.
The overall equation is: carbon dioxide + water + light -> glucose + oxygen.
Photosynthesis supports life by creating food for plants and oxygen for animals and humans.
