Newton's three laws of motion explain how objects move.
First law: an object stays at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by an external force.
Second law: force equals mass times acceleration (F = m * a), meaning heavier objects need more force.
Third law: for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
These laws are used in engineering, sports analysis, and vehicle safety design.
They are foundational ideas in classical mechanics.
